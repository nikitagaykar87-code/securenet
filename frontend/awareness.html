⚖️ Legal Framework (India)

  1. 01

    Information Technology Act, 2000 – The primary legislation governing cyber crimes and electronic commerce in India.

  2. 02

    IT Amendment Act, 2008 – Expanded scope to include data protection, cyber terrorism, and enhanced privacy regulations.

  3. 03

    Section 43 – Outlines penalties for unauthorized system access, data theft, and intentional damage to digital infrastructure.

  4. 04

    Section 66 – Deals with computer-related offenses, hacking, and damaging computer resources with malicious intent.

  5. 05

    Section 66C – Specifically addresses punishment for identity theft and the fraudulent use of electronic signatures or passwords.

  6. 06

    Section 66D – Imposes punishment for cheating by personation while using computer resources or digital communication devices.

  7. 07

    Section 66E – Criminalizes the violation of privacy through the capturing or publishing of private images without consent.

  8. 08

    Section 67 – Prohibits the publishing or transmitting of obscene, sexually explicit, or harmful content in electronic form.

  9. 09

    Section 69 – Grants the Government the power to issue directions for interception or monitoring of information for national security.

  10. 10

    Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 – Modern legislation focused on protecting citizen data privacy and processing rights.
